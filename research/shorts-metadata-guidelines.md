# YouTube Shorts Metadata Guidelines (2025–2026)

**Purpose:** Reference/instruction block for an LLM that generates a **title + description + hashtags + tags** for one vertical AP (Advanced Placement) study explainer Short, given its transcript. Audience: high-school / early-college students. Format: talking-head creator explaining a concept.

**Scope note:** Shorts are ranked *primarily on performance and viewer relevancy* (retention, swipe-away rate, engagement) rather than on metadata. Metadata's job is (1) to earn the click/hold in the first 1–2 seconds via the title, and (2) to help YouTube correctly *categorize* the Short so it reaches AP students in search and the Shorts feed. Optimize the video first; treat metadata as the tie-breaker that decides *who* sees it. Sources: [YouTube Help](https://support.google.com/youtube/answer/12948449?hl=en), [vidIQ Shorts algorithm](https://vidiq.com/blog/post/youtube-shorts-algorithm/), [CRKLR](https://crklr.com/news/how-to-optimise-youtube-shorts-for-seo/).

## 1. TITLES

### Length (data-backed)
- **Hard limit:** 100 characters. Do not use it. ([vidIQ](https://vidiq.com/blog/post/youtube-shorts-titles/))
- **Mobile truncation:** Titles are cut with an ellipsis at **~40 characters** in the Shorts feed. Anything past ~40 chars is invisible before the viewer swipes. ([CRKLR](https://crklr.com/news/how-to-optimise-youtube-shorts-for-seo/), [vidIQ](https://vidiq.com/blog/post/youtube-shorts-titles/))
- **Target:** **20–40 characters / 4–6 words.** A 10,000+ Shorts analysis found this is the highest-performing band once hashtags are excluded (20–40 chars = the largest cluster; 4–6 words = a top cluster). Average trending title is ~51 chars / 8 words — but the *tighter* band performs best. ([TunePocket 10K analysis](https://www.tunepocket.com/youtube-shorts-video-titles/))
- **Practical rule for AP:** keep it **≤ 50 characters**, and make sure the **topic keyword + hook both live in the first 40 characters**.

### Front-loading & keyword placement
- **Put the primary keyword near the front** of the title so it survives truncation and aligns the Short with related long-form/search results. The most important keyword *must* appear in the first ~40 chars. ([vidIQ](https://vidiq.com/blog/post/youtube-shorts-titles/), [CRKLR](https://crklr.com/news/how-to-optimise-youtube-shorts-for-seo/))
- For AP content, the keyword is the **AP course + concept**: e.g. "AP World," "AP Human Geo," "AP Bio," plus the concept ("Demographic Transition," "Krebs Cycle," "Columbian Exchange"). Get the concept name in early — it matches how students actually search ("ap bio krebs cycle explained").

### Hook patterns that work
- **Curiosity gap** — imply an answer the viewer must watch to get. ("The AP Bio topic everyone gets wrong")
- **How / Why / What** questions — direct, search-aligned, high intent. ("Why did the Columbian Exchange matter?")
- **Direct address ("you") / POV** — speaks to the student. ("POV: it's the night before the AP exam")
- **Numbers / list format** — underused and effective; only **~12.8%** of Shorts titles use numbers, so they stand out. ("3 rubric words that get you the point")
- **Clarity over hype** — only **~1.9%** of trending Shorts titles use "power words" (sensational/clickbait). Winning Shorts prioritize a clear, accurate promise. Do **not** write clickbait the video can't pay off. ([TunePocket](https://www.tunepocket.com/youtube-shorts-video-titles/))
- The title should create a **curiosity loop** and jump straight to the compelling part — no throat-clearing. ([miraflow](https://miraflow.ai/blog/youtube-shorts-best-practices-2026-complete-guide))

### Emoji
- **Optional, sparing.** ~32% of Shorts titles use an emoji (higher in Sports/Music). For an educational/authority niche, use **0–1** relevant emoji max, and never let it push the keyword past char 40. Prefer no emoji if it costs you keyword real estate. ([TunePocket](https://www.tunepocket.com/youtube-shorts-video-titles/))

### 10 title templates for AP explainers
(`{AP}` = course e.g. "AP World"; `{concept}` = topic e.g. "Demographic Transition")

1. `{concept}, explained in {N} seconds ({AP})` — e.g. "Demographic Transition explained in 60s (AP Human Geo)"
2. `The {AP} topic everyone gets wrong` — e.g. "The AP Bio topic everyone gets wrong"
3. `Why {phenomenon}? ({AP})` — e.g. "Why did empires collapse? (AP World)"
4. `How to actually understand {concept}` — e.g. "How to actually understand the Krebs cycle"
5. `{N} things to know about {concept}` — e.g. "3 things to know about the Columbian Exchange"
6. `{concept} in one sentence ({AP})` — e.g. "Osmosis in one sentence (AP Bio)"
7. `POV: {exam situation}` — e.g. "POV: the AP World DBQ just dropped"
8. `Stop confusing {A} and {B}` — e.g. "Stop confusing weathering and erosion"
9. `The fastest way to remember {concept}` — e.g. "The fastest way to remember the unit circle"
10. `{concept}? Here's the only part that matters` — e.g. "Enzymes? Here's the only part that matters"

Bonus: `Get the {rubric/FRQ} point: {concept}` — e.g. "Get the FRQ point: push and pull factors"

## 2. DESCRIPTIONS

### Do descriptions still matter for Shorts in 2026?
- **Yes — secondarily.** YouTube ranks Shorts mainly on performance/relevancy, not metadata, so descriptions won't "save" a weak video. But they still (a) feed **search/Google discovery**, (b) help YouTube **categorize** the Short toward the right audience, and (c) surface the first hashtags above the title. Treating the description as optional leaves discovery on the table. ([CRKLR](https://crklr.com/news/how-to-optimise-youtube-shorts-for-seo/), [NEXORA](https://nexora-ai.org/blog/youtube-description-seo-2026), [InfluenceFlow](https://influenceflow.io/resources/optimized-video-titles-and-descriptions-the-complete-2026-youtube-seo-guide/))

### Length
- **Limit:** 5,000 characters (irrelevant here). ([YouTube Help](https://support.google.com/youtube/answer/12948449?hl=en))
- **Target for Shorts:** a focused **~150–500 characters** beats a padded wall of text. ([CRKLR](https://crklr.com/news/how-to-optimise-youtube-shorts-for-seo/))
- **Only the first ~125 characters** show in previews / above the "…more" fold — that is effectively the whole description for most viewers. ([CRKLR](https://crklr.com/news/how-to-optimise-youtube-shorts-for-seo/), [YouTube Help](https://support.google.com/youtube/answer/12948449?hl=en))

### First 1–2 lines (the only part shown)
- Lead with a **natural-language sentence containing the AP course + concept keyword and the core promise** — mirror the title's intent, don't copy it verbatim. ([YouTube Help](https://support.google.com/youtube/answer/12948449?hl=en), [miraflow templates](https://miraflow.ai/blog/youtube-shorts-titles-descriptions-2026-templates))
- Template (tutorial/explainer): `Line 1: "{concept} explained for {AP} — the simple version."` / `Line 2: "In this Short: {the 1 key idea / why it earns points}."`
- Example: "The Demographic Transition Model explained for AP Human Geography — the simple version. In this Short: the 5 stages and what actually happens to birth and death rates."

### Keyword placement
- Primary keyword (course + concept) in the **first sentence**, naturally. Add 1–2 **semantic variants** ("explained," "for beginners," "AP exam review," "unit X") in the body — this helps clustering. Do not keyword-stuff. ([CRKLR](https://crklr.com/news/how-to-optimise-youtube-shorts-for-seo/), [NEXORA](https://nexora-ai.org/blog/youtube-description-seo-2026), [YouTube Help](https://support.google.com/youtube/answer/12948449?hl=en))

### CTA patterns
- One light CTA, engagement-first (comments/shares are weighted above likes for distribution — see §5): "Follow for daily AP [course] explainers." / "Comment the unit you want next." / "Save this before the exam." Keep it to **one short line**, after the value.

### Links
- Fine to include (channel, study guide, playlist), but **below the fold** — never in the first 125 chars, and never before the keyword line. Links do nothing for the Shorts feed itself; they're for the small share that expands the description.

### Hashtags in the description
- Put the hashtag set on its own line at the **end** of the description (see §3 for the set). The **first 3** description hashtags render as clickable links above the title.

## 3. HASHTAGS

### How many
- **3–5 total.** This is the consensus sweet spot. Only the **first 3** hashtags in the description display above the title, so the first 3 are the ones that matter most. ([hashtagtools](https://hashtagtools.io/blog/youtube-shorts-hashtags-title-vs-description-2026), [CRKLR](https://crklr.com/news/how-to-optimise-youtube-shorts-for-seo/))
- **Penalty limits:** exceeding **15** hashtags can make YouTube ignore all of them on that video (some sources cite a hard 60 cap where all are dropped). Stay at 3–5. ([hashtagtools](https://hashtagtools.io/blog/youtube-shorts-hashtags-title-vs-description-2026), [CRKLR](https://crklr.com/news/how-to-optimise-youtube-shorts-for-seo/))

### Placement: description, not title
- Put hashtags in the **description**, not the title. Keeping them out of the title preserves your ~40-char keyword window; the first 3 description hashtags still surface above the title as links. ([hashtagtools](https://hashtagtools.io/blog/youtube-shorts-hashtags-title-vs-description-2026))

### Is #Shorts still needed in 2026?
- **Yes — include #Shorts.** It signals the format to YouTube's recommendation engine and helps land on the Shorts shelf. (Note: it appears in only ~17% of *titles*, but the guidance is to keep it in the *description*.) ([hashtagtools](https://hashtagtools.io/blog/youtube-shorts-hashtags-title-vs-description-2026), [CRKLR](https://crklr.com/news/how-to-optimise-youtube-shorts-for-seo/))

### Mix: broad + niche
- Formula: **#Shorts (format) + 2–3 niche/topic tags + 1 broad-education tag.** Put the most specific/niche tags in the first 3 slots (highest visibility), with #Shorts included. ([hashtagtools](https://hashtagtools.io/blog/youtube-shorts-hashtags-title-vs-description-2026))

### Example hashtag sets (AP study)
- **AP World History:** `#APWorld #APWorldHistory #Shorts #APExam #studytok`
- **AP Human Geography:** `#APHumanGeography #APHuG #Shorts #APExam #studygram`
- **AP Biology:** `#APBio #APBiology #Shorts #APExam #biology`
- **AP US History:** `#APUSH #APUSHistory #Shorts #APExam #history`
- **AP Psychology:** `#APPsych #APPsychology #Shorts #APExam #psychology`
- **Generic fallback:** `#AP #APExam #Shorts #studytips #{coursehashtag}`

Rule: always include the **course's own community hashtag** (e.g. #APHuG, #APUSH, #APBio) — that's where students self-organize — plus #Shorts, plus #APExam, and trim to 3–5.

## 4. TAGS (backend keyword tags)

- **Still worth ~2 minutes, but minor for Shorts.** YouTube says titles/descriptions are stronger signals than tags, and that's *more* true for Shorts, where the feed is engagement-driven. Tags act as a **context anchor** that raises the algorithm's confidence in categorizing the Short and reinforces the title keywords. They are **not** a discovery driver. ([Hollyland — Shorts tags](https://www.hollyland.com/blog/topics/youtube-shorts-tags), [Joyspace](https://joyspace.ai/youtube-shorts-tags-dead-or-vital-2026), [InfluenceFlow metadata](https://influenceflow.io/resources/youtube-metadata-and-descriptions-the-complete-2026-creators-guide-to-video-discoverability/))
- **How many:** **5–8** precise tags. ([Joyspace](https://joyspace.ai/youtube-shorts-tags-dead-or-vital-2026))
- **What kind — "specific → broad" pyramid:**
  1. **Exact concept** (long-tail): "ap bio krebs cycle explained," "demographic transition model"
  2. **Course/niche:** "ap biology," "ap human geography," "ap exam review"
  3. **Broad:** "study tips," "high school biology," "exam prep"
  - Favor **long-tail phrases** over single words; include common phrasings students type. ([Joyspace](https://joyspace.ai/youtube-shorts-tags-dead-or-vital-2026))

## 5. SHORTS ALGORITHM SIGNALS (2026)

Order of importance for distribution — the video, not the metadata, wins here. Metadata should support these, never fight them.

1. **Hook in the first 3 seconds (make-or-break).** ~50–60% of drop-off happens in the first 3s; the title/first frame must deliver the payoff-promise immediately. Aim for **>70% intro retention** (viewers who make it past 3s). ([Opus](https://www.opus.pro/blog/ideal-youtube-shorts-length-format-retention), [vidIQ](https://vidiq.com/blog/post/youtube-shorts-algorithm/))
2. **Completion / retention rate.** Target **>60% completion** for sub-30s Shorts; **70%+** is strong, **80%+** elite. Shorts hitting **~70%+ completion** get aggressive promotion. ([Opus](https://www.opus.pro/blog/ideal-youtube-shorts-length-format-retention), [CRKLR](https://crklr.com/news/how-to-optimise-youtube-shorts-for-seo/))
3. **Loops / replays.** Every loop counts as a view and is heavily weighted; even a **~10% replay rate** meaningfully boosts distribution. Design a seamless last-frame→first-frame loop. ([Opus](https://www.opus.pro/blog/ideal-youtube-shorts-length-format-retention), [vidIQ](https://vidiq.com/blog/post/youtube-shorts-algorithm/))
4. **Low swipe-away rate / watch time per impression.** 2026 weighs watch time per impression over raw swipe counts. ([vidIQ](https://vidiq.com/blog/post/youtube-shorts-algorithm/))
5. **Comments & shares > likes** for distribution — engineer a reason to comment (ask a question, invite the next-topic request). ([CRKLR](https://crklr.com/news/how-to-optimise-youtube-shorts-for-seo/))
6. **Burned-in captions / on-screen text.** ~70%+ watch on mute; captioned Shorts see **~12–25% higher retention/completion**, and YouTube indexes caption text for search. Always ship burned-in captions. ([Opus captions](https://www.opus.pro/blog/youtube-shorts-caption-subtitle-best-practices), [Opus length](https://www.opus.pro/blog/ideal-youtube-shorts-length-format-retention))
7. **Length.** Retention sweet spot **~15–45s** for explainers (tips 15–20s, tutorials 25–40s). Under 10s feels incomplete; over ~45s drops off unless exceptional. ([Opus](https://www.opus.pro/blog/ideal-youtube-shorts-length-format-retention))
8. **Explore → exploit rollout + consistency.** New Shorts get a seed-audience test, then broaden if signals are good; consistent posting/volume helps the channel's baseline. ([vidIQ](https://vidiq.com/blog/post/youtube-shorts-algorithm/))

> Metadata implication: the **title is a retention lever** (it sets the 3-second promise), not just SEO. Write the title as the thing that makes a student *stop scrolling and stay*.

## GENERATION RULES (do / don't, with limits)

Given a video transcript, produce **title + description + 3–5 hashtags + 5–8 tags**.

**TITLE**
- DO keep it **≤ 50 characters** (hard cap 100). Put the **AP course + concept keyword AND the hook within the first 40 characters.**
- DO use one hook pattern: curiosity gap, How/Why/What, a number, POV, or direct "you."
- DO write clear, accurate promises the video actually delivers.
- DON'T use clickbait/power-word hype, ALL CAPS spam, or more than **1** emoji (prefer 0).
- DON'T put hashtags in the title.
- DON'T bury the concept name after char 40.

**DESCRIPTION**
- DO write **~150–500 characters**. Put the **AP course + concept keyword + core promise in the first 125 characters** (line 1).
- DO make line 1–2 a natural sentence that mirrors the title's intent without copying it word-for-word; state the one key takeaway.
- DO add 1–2 semantic keyword variants ("explained," "AP exam review," "unit X") in the body.
- DO add **one** short engagement CTA (comment/follow/save), after the value.
- DON'T keyword-stuff, don't lead with links, don't exceed ~500 chars, don't waste line 1 on a CTA or hashtag.

**HASHTAGS**
- DO output **3–5** hashtags on their own line at the **end of the description**.
- DO make the **first 3** the highest-value: include the **course community tag** (e.g. #APHuG/#APUSH/#APBio), **#Shorts**, and **#APExam**; add 1–2 niche/subject tags.
- DON'T exceed 5 (never approach 15), don't put hashtags in the title, don't use irrelevant trending tags.

**TAGS**
- DO output **5–8** backend tags as a "specific → broad" pyramid: exact concept (long-tail) → course/niche → broad study terms.
- DO phrase as students search ("ap bio krebs cycle explained").
- DON'T pad beyond 8, don't use single generic words only, don't repeat the same tag in different case.

**GLOBAL**
- DO derive the concept keyword and the one key takeaway from the transcript; match student search intent.
- DO assume the video already has a strong 3-second hook, a seamless loop, and burned-in captions — write the title to reinforce that hook.
- DON'T let any metadata field promise something the transcript doesn't deliver.
