Your body makes its own body weight in this molecule every single day — and you'd die in seconds without it.

It's called ATP, and it's the energy currency of every living cell. This is core AP Biology.

Think of ATP like a charged battery. It has three phosphate groups, and that last bond is packed with energy.

When your cell needs power, it snaps off that phosphate — turning ATP into ADP — and releases the energy to do work, like contracting a muscle.

Then your mitochondria recharge it right back to ATP. Charge, use, recharge — billions of times a day. That's you, running on tiny batteries.
