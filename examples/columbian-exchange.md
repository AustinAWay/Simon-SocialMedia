Here's a wild fact: the tomato in your pasta and the chili in your curry didn't exist in Europe or Asia until 500 years ago.

That's the Columbian Exchange — when Columbus crossed the Atlantic in fourteen ninety-two, he kicked off the biggest swap of plants, animals, and diseases in human history.

Going east: potatoes, corn, and tomatoes. These crops were so packed with calories they caused populations to boom across Europe, Africa, and Asia.

Going west: horses, wheat, and cattle — but also smallpox, which wiped out up to ninety percent of Indigenous populations in the Americas.

So next time you eat fries, remember: that potato is a souvenir from one of the most world-changing events on your AP exam.
